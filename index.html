The Factors that Impact MLB Fan Attendance Today

By Ford Anderson

The 2025 MLB season had massive attendance shifts across the league. 8 different teams saw at least a 10% change fan attendance per game from 2024 into 2025.

The 2025 season had three teams see attendance rise by at least 10%. The Mets led the league with a surge of 36.7% more fans at each home game compared to 2024. The Tigers followed in second with a boost of 29.9%. There was nearly a 20% difference between the Tigers and the third ranked team ranked according to this data, the San Francisco Giants. The Giants had an increase of 10.5%.

The 2025 season also had five teams see attendance drop by at least 10%. The Tampa Bay Rays had the biggest decline at 41.2% , nearly twice as big of a dip in attendance compared to the second and third placed teams according to this data, the Cardinals and Orioles. The Cardinals had 21.8% fewer fans at every home game compared to 2024. The Orioles saw a drop of 20.9%. The Athletics were in fourth with a decrease of 16.7%. The fifth team was the Pirates with 11.4% fewer fans.

There are two new stadiums in 2025. The Rays played their 2025 home games in Steinbrenner Field that has a capacity of 11,026. Tampa Bay could not use its home stadium Tropicana Field because Hurricane Milton Damaged it. The Athletics played in Sutter Health Park with a limit of 14,014 fans, while the team awaits their new stadium in Las Vegas.

There are many factors that impact fan attendance every season. Steven Zymkowitz, the Senior Director of Ticket Operations for the Dodgers, says that different elements impact each team uniquely.

“I think MLB with their 30 different teams and subsidiaries have all have different environments for those 30 teams,” Zymkowitz said. “Every Team has a business that looks a litte bit different, so I do think that it can be a little bit skewed by market and success can be different for different clubs.”

While there are so many different things that can impact each team, team success is “pretty dynamic across the league,” according to Zymkowitz.

The Mets for example, made it to the 2024 NLCS which created buzz around the team. SS Jose Iglesias performed his hit song “OMG” on the field after a game. They signed Juan Soto to a record 15-year $765 million deal in the offseason which brought excitement to Mets fans.

Zymkowitz says that the MLB is unique because every team can succeed in their own way.

“So teams like the Brewers, the Guardians, and the Rays over the years have been famous for not necessarily having high payrolls or big star players, but still churning out success from a wins loss perspective,” Zymkowitz said.

Another aspect that can influence fan attendance is the use of promotions. Teams that have stars from an area tend to embrace the culture of those players for heritage nights to bring more fans in seats. The Red Sox hosted celebrations for more than 20 different cultures and identities during last season.

Other “super key comments” include demand, market, pricing, and availability, according to Zymkowitz.

Teams can also host promotions through special nights that give away items like bobbleheads or other memorabilia to the first fans that show up. The Tigers for example on May 10, 2025, gave away Tarik Skubal mystery bobbleheads to the first 15,000 fans that showed up.

Another major change in the MLB is the personality of some of the biggest stars. There were many unwritten rules in the game known as “our National Pastime” that frowned upon players from showing emotion. Today, the league allows its stars like Jazz Chisolm and Ronald Acuña Jr. to be themselves through fashion and emotion on the field.

The MLB has changed many rules in the past few years to speed up the pace of play. The biggest rule change for the 2026 season is the addition of the automatic ball strike system. These rule changes have made baseball more popular and have sparked fan interest, according to Zymkowitz.

The faster games have caused interest of the game to continue to get younger. Some fans are content creators who post on social media that gives more attention to the games. Younger fans are a positive thing since the game will become more accessible for the next generation, according to Zymkowitz.

MLB also has played games internationally to grow baseball fandom around the world This international reach has also allowed the league to continue growing its television audience. The 2025 World Series had more worldwide viewers than the NBA Finals.

The MLB sits in a great spot, according to Zymkowitz.

The collective bargaining agreement between the players association and the owners ends after the 2026 season.
